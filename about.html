About the Interactive Bloch Sphere Visualizer

The Interactive Bloch Sphere Visualizer is a web-based educational and research-oriented tool designed to visualize the geometry of single-qubit quantum states and their evolution on the Bloch sphere.

This tool provides an intuitive bridge between the mathematical formalism of quantum mechanics and its geometric interpretation, enabling learners and researchers to explore quantum states, unitary operations, and measurement outcomes interactively.

Key features:
  • Interactive 3D Bloch sphere with mouse-based rotation
  • Real-time visualization of qubit state vectors
  • Polar and azimuthal angle computation
  • Standard quantum gates (I, X, Y, Z, H, S, T, and rotations)
  • Trajectory tracing during unitary evolution
  • Measurement probabilities and state representation

Intended Use

This visualizer is intended for:

  • Undergraduate and postgraduate teaching in quantum computing
  • Self-learning and conceptual understanding of qubit states
  • Demonstrations in workshops, FDPs, and seminars
  • Pedagogical and exploratory research in quantum information science
